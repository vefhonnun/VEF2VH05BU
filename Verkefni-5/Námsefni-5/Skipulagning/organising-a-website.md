# Planning

Organizing a single-product website requires a focused structure that removes distractions and guides users directly toward a single goal: purchasing your "hero" product. Unlike multi-category stores, you should treat your entire site as a high-converting sales funnel. [1, 2, 3, 4] 

1. Define Your Core Site Structure
Even with only one product, you need a few essential pages to build trust and meet legal requirements: [2, 5] 

* Homepage (Main Sales Page): This is your primary landing page. It should act as a comprehensive showcase for your product.
* Product Page: A detailed breakdown with technical specs, all available variants (colors, sizes), and the primary "Add to Cart" button.
* About Us: Tell your brand story, explain the problem your product solves, and share your mission to build emotional connections.
* Contact Us: Provide a clear way for customers to reach out via email, form, or chat.
* Policy Pages: Mandatory for trust and legal compliance; include Shipping, Returns, and Terms of Service. [2, 5, 6, 7, 8] 

2. Optimize the Homepage Layout
For a single-product store, the homepage is often the only page a customer needs to see. Organize it into these high-impact sections: [1, 2, 9] 

* Hero Section: Capture attention in under 5 seconds with a high-quality product image or video, a benefit-driven headline, and a clear Call-to-Action (CTA) like "Shop Now".
* Problem/Solution Section: Explicitly state the "pain point" your audience faces and how your product is the ultimate fix.
* Feature Showcase: Use icons and short text blocks to highlight 5–7 key benefits (e.g., "Eco-friendly," "Waterproof").
* Social Proof: Embed customer reviews, star ratings, or user-generated content from TikTok or Instagram to build immediate credibility.
* FAQ Section: Address common objections or questions (e.g., "How long does shipping take?") to remove barriers to purchase. [1, 2, 5, 10, 11] 

3. Simplify Navigation and Checkout
Reduce the number of clicks required to buy by streamlining the user journey: [7, 11] 

* Minimalist Header: Keep the menu sleek. Focus on a few links like "Home," "Reviews," and "Contact," with the product prominently featured.
* Sticky "Add to Cart": Ensure a CTA button is always visible, either above the fold or as a sticky bar that follows the user as they scroll.
* Direct Checkout: Consider skipping the "Cart" page entirely and redirecting users straight to "Checkout" to minimize friction. [2, 7, 10, 12, 13] 

4. Final Check & Testing
Before launching, verify these technical details: [2, 5] 

* Mobile Excellence: Over 50% of traffic is mobile. Ensure all buttons are easy to tap and images scale correctly.
* Page Speed: Use the Google PageSpeed Insights tool to ensure your high-quality visuals aren't slowing down the site.
* Trust Signals: Confirm SSL certificates are active and payment gateways (PayPal, Stripe) are functional.
