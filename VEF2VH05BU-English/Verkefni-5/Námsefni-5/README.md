# Website Preparation and Planning

When starting to design a website, you need to plan the site's content and how interactions with users should work. The benefits of this workflow are significant.

- Contractor and client make an agreement
  - website content (text and imagery) is ready
  - navigation structure and flowchart are complete
  - a prototype is available
  - color palette and typography are defined

> If preparation is lacking, there is a considerable risk of misunderstanding and rework. The contractor ends up under time pressure and the client may refuse to pay overtime.

The next step is user interface design (UI Design, _Prototype_):

- Look and feel: The design should be visually appealing and align with your brand.
- User experience (UX): Ensure users can easily understand and use the app. This includes considering navigation, buttons, and other interactions.

Testing and feedback:

- User testing: Let real users test the prototype and provide feedback.
- Improvements: Use the feedback to improve the design and solve issues that arise.

Final design:

When you are satisfied with the prototype and have received positive feedback, you can move on to designing the final version with all details.
The design process is often iterative, where you may need to go back and improve various elements as you gain more insight and feedback.
